Day 13 Speaking

Posted on 2018-08-07

【P1】日常类：绿植

强调绿植，而不是植物呢？因为在很多外国人眼里，flowers are not plants，plants特指绿色植物。所以答题的时候尽量避免提到花。

Do you keep plants at home?

Well, I used to. I bought 3 bonsais and several lovely succulents on a whim last year. But they all died as a result of excessive watering.

What plant did you grow when you were young?

This may surprise you, but every Chinese citizen is obliged to plant at least 1 tree per year, that’s why we used to go on a tree-planting trip organized by school every year. I planted at least 10 saplings. But I have no idea what became of them.

Do you know anything about growing a plant?

I know nothing about it. Come to think of it, all the potted plants constantly died last year due to my negligence. Don’t worry, I’m not planning to grow anything.

Do Chinese people send plants as gifts?

We sometimes do, yeah. Potted plants are well-liked housewarming gifts. They are practical too, considering they absorb carbon-dioxide and formaldehyde, right?

【P1】日常类：新食物

foodie们的专属题。你尝遍全球了吗？要积累一些外国食物的名称和特征哦。当然还有形容东西好吃和难吃的形容词。

Have you ever tried foreign food?

I just did the other day when a backpacker friend came back from France with canned snails. Well, let’s just say, I threw it away the second I opened it.

Do you like to try new food?

I’m always ready to embrace new things in life and trying new food is one of my things, esp. when I travel to new places. Exotic food never fails to fascinate me.

What kinds of new food have you tried recently?

Well, other than the snails, I tried Tom Yum Goong, a hot and sour Thai soup, pretty much a mishmash of sea food and spices like lemongrass and chili peppers. Sounds appetizing right?

What kinds of foreign food are popular in your country?

American fast food restaurants are a dime a dozen in any Chinese city where fried chicken, French fries and hamburgers are served. Japanese delicacies like sushi and sashimi have been new favorite too.

Do you like any food from the countries near China?

I tried sukiyaki, the Japanese hotpot recently and I can’t get enough of it. It’s basically a dish consisting of thin slices of meat, tofu, and vegetables cooked in soy sauce and sugar. What’s most interesting about it is that dips are raw eggs. Yum!

【P2】事件类：帮助他人

Describe an occasion when you helped a person

  • What the situation was
  • Who you helped
  • How you helped him/her
  • And explain how you felt after helping them.
  1. 本题的A是an occasion，描述一个场合；B是 when you helped a person, 限制内容。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 简述事件发生的时间、地点、参与人物，点出需要帮助的方面
    • 详述帮忙的经过
    • 总结和感受，适当升华
  3. 时态建议：过去时，没得跑。
  4. 其他：本题是最标准的事件类话题，只需要按照现行后续描述事件即可。注意叙事要突出细节。

So I’m a backpacker and I’ve been so many places in my life and time, but I can never forget last summer when I finally comprehended how surreal it feels to save lives. I rented a Harley and drove on the Delhi-Gurgoan Highway like a streak of lightening with the beat of Eminem. Cool, right?

Then a white car loomed on the edge of the road which seemed not ready to pull off. I just had a hunch that there was something wrong cause I saw its blinkers were on before I parked behind it and drank some water from my water bladder. Then I went closer to the car, waiting to check on the driver.

I had never seen someone suffer like that in my entire life. there he was, a twenty-something in a white jacket, coughing, wheezing and shuffling for something. Apparently, he was heavily out of breath and desperately in need of help. I soon recalled that an asthma can be alleviated with water. I saw it on tv and I couldn’t imagine how it was like to have the muscles around the airways contracting. Then I took my extra bottle of water from my backpack and offered it to him right away. He struggled to open the door, staggered out, grabbed the bottle and gulped it down. Then he hobbled further and threw up. He was feeling better, I could sense that.

I helped him back to his car and asked him if he needed to go to the hospital. He replied with a voice flat and tired. Then I stayed with him for another 15 minutes until he said he was a lot better and could drive again. I took out of my water bladder, handed it to him and said, “In case you need it later” before I walked to my Harley. I noticed when I drove past him that he wanted to say something but couldn’t utter it because he was too feeble. I got it and I didn’t need to hear it.

【P2】事件类：感兴趣的历史

Describe a historical era(/period) you are interested in

  • When this era was
  • How you learned about it
  • What it is famous for
  • What happened during that period
  • And explain why you are interested in this era
  1. 本题的A是a historical era/period，描述一个历史时期；B是you are interested in, 限制特征。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 简述这一历史时期的基本信息，大致起讫时间等
    • 选择这一历史时期有趣的一两个地方详讲
    • 总结和感受，适当升华
  3. 时态建议：讲历史时期的基本情况和事件用过去时，述及后人的评价可用完成时和现在时。
  4. 其他：本题需要注意留余地，因为感兴趣的历史之所以还能继续让你感兴趣，大致上就是因为还有你不了解的地方。另外本题在准备的时候，资料比重是比较大的，一定要做好充分的准备。

Just the other day, I bumped into a picture when I was browsing news sites which showed some early morning bathers at a hotel pool in Las Vegas stop to watch the mushroom cloud of an atomic detonation at a test site about dozens of miles from the city. It was taken in the 1970. There’s nothing more appalling than nuclear bombs. But I’m so curious about how people dealt with that primal fear on a daily basis back then.

It followed by the report that throughout the 1950s, atomic detonations brought countless tourists to Sin City — and helped make it what it is today. The threat of nuclear annihilation throughout the Cold War, its early years especially, might conjure up images of schoolchildren being told to “duck and cover” under their desks in the event of an attack, which one can get hint of easily by watching movies about that period. However, it soon struck me that in classic American fashion, fear wasn’t the only reaction. In addition to the terrified people who built bomb shelters in their backyards, there were also plenty of enterprising individuals who saw the silver lining, or should I say green lining, of the Atomic Age.

In the 1940s, the United States government began its first batch of nuclear testing in the desert areas near to Las Vegas. And soon the Soviet Union followed suit. Escalations were rampant and people in an unprecedented fear. But the pictures and report I read about showed the other side of the story. To take the edge off the tremendous horror, people tried to have some fun of theirs.

I was literally shocked by how resilient people sought happiness through embracing tough times. Compared to that interesting period, the adversities my generation encounter are all but nothing.

【P2】事件类：花钱少的外出

Describe a special day out（a day out/a special day which did not cost too much）

  • What day it was
  • Where you went on that day
  • What you did
  • Who you went out with
  • And explain why it was special to you
  1. 本题的A是a day out，描述一次外出经历；B是special, which did not cost too much, 限制特征。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 简述这一外出的时间、地点、人物，引出省钱多
    • 详述外出的过程、省钱的经过和结果
    • 总结和感受，点出special，适当升华
  3. 时态建议：没商量，过去时。将special如果上升到对人生的影响，可以是完成时或现在时。
  4. 其他：本题是典型的记叙文，讲故事，注意故事的细节和生动性。另外，没花多少钱也是一个重点，需要单独提出，最好独立成段。Special也需要体现在外出的经过中。

So last Sunday, I was still in my sweet dreams when my cousin Jerry bombarded me with voice messages on WeChat, urging me to get dressed and go on a picnic with him and my 70-year-old grandma. Wait, did he just mention something about taking our grandma, the lovely old lady out, first time this year? How could I miss it, right? I soon got up and went straight to the nearby store and purchased the lamb kabab, marshmallows and beverages as Jerry suggested, which cost me only 50 rmb.

It was a typical picnic. Jerry drove us to a riverside park in a suburb. We unfolded the cloth, unloaded our snacks and got ready to be kissed by the brisk breeze of morning wind. I hadn’t had that guilty pleasure of binge eating cheap junk food for too long that I devoured 10 kebabs, 2 bags of marshmallows and 2 cans of soda. It’s good to see my granny taken aback by my sound belch.

Plus, it was nice talking with my grandma and my cousin. We chatted about a lot of stuff, from my stressful study to my cousin’s new job, to my grandma’s recent medical treatment. We had a great talk. My grandma is a loving and caring old lady Who loves spinning yarns and talking about how frugal their life in the past was, how we should save as much as can. And of course, the rehashed version of the old story of how she was adopted and how her biological brother would wade miles and miles to get her back home to stay for a day and send her back. We were blessed to be able to hear that story time and time again.

【每日表达】句型、习语、小幽默

be all eyes

be all ears

  • 这两个习语很生动地表达出了全神贯注地看或听的场面、动作。当需要全神贯注看一个精彩绝伦的表演或惊心动魄的电影时，是不是希望自己全身上下长满了眼睛，不错过任何一个细节？用all eyes就可以表达出这种感受。相类似的，听一段美妙的音乐或富有感染力的演讲时，也希望自己全身上下长满了耳朵，听到每个乐器的呼吸，听到演讲者的抑扬顿挫。用all ears就能表达。记住，这两个习语用作表语是最常见的，因此记得在前面加be动词。
  • Children were all ears when Granny spun yarns.
  • Once Michael appeared on the stage, the audience was all eyes.

play fast and loose (with)

  • 有些电影电视剧小说等文艺作品在处理历史类题材时，往往会做大量的改编，与史实有重大出入。这当然有好有坏。对于历史或任何事实的“戏说”、改编、不认真看待，就叫play fast and loose with something. 当然，这个习语不光只能用在文艺作品中，对任何东西不认真看待都可以用。情感倾向上，它用作中性偏贬义居多。
  • The film is very entertaining even though it plays fast and loose with the historical facts.

In reality, love probably goes on with breaks and interruptions. First, it’s difficult to suppose that one can experience anything continuously. Sleep interrupts wakefulness and sleep itself is interrupted by dreams and nightmares. The feeling one has for his lover during wakefulness may be blotted out or intensified by sleep. In either case, the feeling changes.

When one is awake, he cannot fix his eyes for his attention constantly on a single object. He must blink, if nothing else. More likely he will look to something else for variety or from necessity.
