Day 12 Speaking

Posted on 2018-08-06

【P1】梦想职业

中在儿时与现在两个阶段的梦想职业及其变化的过程和原因。选取自己两个阶段的想法，并有一定拓展即可。

What was your dream job when you were a child?

I used to dream of being an astronaut when I was in elementary school. The mysteriously boundless universe never failed to fascinate me I once asked my parents to buy this delicate diorama of the solar system and plenty of sci-fi books.

Have you changed your mind on your dream job?

I have, pathetically. Not until I entered college did I realize that it’s not realistic to strive for the stars. Instead, I chose to study aviation as my major and wanna devote to the aviation industry when I graduate.

What kinds of jobs are popular in China?

Well-paid jobs are popular, obviously. The lawyer and the physician rank the most popular two. Some graduates long to become a civil servant or a teacher too. Stability seems the second crucial factor here.

Have you had a full-time or a part-time job before?

I once was a part-time waiter in a local Chinese restaurant. Not until I got the job did I realize how hard it is to make a good money as a result of the latest recession.

【P1】事件类：省钱

开源节流，省钱是很重要的理财手段。注意总结自己现在和儿时的省钱经历、方法等。

Did you save money when you were young?

I didn’t, even if I wanted to. I mean, I got a weekly allowance as scanty as 2 rmb for my stationery, books and the like. I had to bum some snacks off my classmates back then.

Did you ever give money to other children?

I didn’t give any money to any kids until I was 18 and supposed to send red packets to younger generation. I myself couldn’t save any, what was the point of giving it out?

Do you think parents should teach children to save money?

They surely should. I don’t think any parent would want a prodigal son, cause they never return. Plus, personal financing is such a critical skill for anyone living in this society with high inflation.

Do parents give children pocket money in China?

They do. Many parents give children weekly or monthly allowances in order to guide their kids to save money and spend money in a sensible way. Although it’s wishful thinking sometimes.

【P2】事件类：庆祝重要事件

Describe an important event that you celebrated

  • What it was
  • Where it happened
  • Why it was important
  • How people felt about it
  1. 本题的A是event，描述事件；B是important, that you celebrated, 限制内容。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 介绍这次庆祝的源起、时间地点、准备工作等
    • 详述庆祝的经过
    • 叙述自己和其他参与者的感受，总结
  3. 时态建议：本题应主要使用过去时，讲到该庆祝的规律性事项时使用一般现在时。述及对现在的影响用现在完成时。
  4. 其他：一次庆祝事件用标准的记叙手法展开即可，但要尽量穿插一些细节和人物心理活动描写，避免流水账。

Last month, my brother and I threw our parents a party, a grand function, I would call it,to celebrate their silver jubilee. They actually had turned down the idea when I came it up to them 2 months ago, saying that it was not worth all the hassle. Then yeah, it would be a surprise.

My brother proposed decorating our living room with a retro 90s twist. We affixed cheesy posters of Andy Lau – my mom’s idol in her younger years – to the wall, placed bouquets of lilies – the flowers my dad used to send Mom when they were in college when everything was still up in the air, and so on. I called and invited everyone on their phone book as well.

When they came back home from the date in a fancy restaurant that we urged, everything was in order. I can never forget their faces when they stepped in, holding hands, mouth agape, before my mom complained about her make-up. The best part of that party is when Dad renewed his very wedding vows 25 years before in which he stated how he met Mom in college, how they had their first kiss in front of the class during their graduation trip, how they decided to marry but skip a wedding. I can’t believe it brought tears to my mom’s eyes, once again. Then she begrudged us squandering money on the tedious posters and things, with a broad smile.

【P2】事件类：听说的轶闻

Describe an impressive/memorable story you heard from someone

  • What this story was
  • When you heard about it
  • Who told you this story
  • And explain why you were impressed/memorable by it
  1. 本题的A是story，讲故事；B是impressive/memorable, you heard from someone,限制故事特征和来源。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 做好讲故事的预备，比如从谁听说的，故事起因是什么
    • 详述故事
    • 讲述故事的有趣或令人印象深刻的点
  3. 时态建议：从别人那里听说的过程应当是过去时，而讲故事的部分则需根据故事本身的需要调整时态。
  4. 其他：这是叠加形态的事件类题目，也就是说一定会讲到两个或两个以上时间节点的事情，一定注意时态的运用。另外，不要忽视了这道题的故事特征要求，也就是impressive/memorable，那种没有情节没有转折没有高潮的故事就不要讲了。

I’d like to tell you an anecdote of the eccentric physicist Albert Einstein that I heard from my roommate, a gossipy geek, who’s been a source of news concerning science and technology. But this one is about Einstein, but has nothing to do with physics.

By the 1920s, Albert Einstein was already regarded as the most famous physicist alive. At the end of the year when the brilliant scientist was touring Japan for a lecture series for which he was paid 2,000 pounds by his Japanese publisher, Einstein learned that he had been awarded the Nobel Prize in physics on the way from Europe. News traveled fast and his lectures gathered crowds of thousands of Japanese looking to catch a glimpse of a Nobel laureate in person. One night, exhausted by all the publicity, Einstein was gathering his thoughts in his room at a normal hotel in Tokyo. A messenger knocked on his door with a delivery. But the messenger refused to accept a tip, in line with local practice. Instead, Einstein handed the man two notes, the hotel’s stationery scribbled with his words of wisdom on how to live a happy life.

The first note simply stated, “a quiet and modest life brings more joy than a pursuit of success bound with constant unrest.” And the second contained a familiar aphorism: “where there’s a will, there’s a way.”

What’s most incredible about the story is that the two notes were sold for $1.8 million combined at a Jerusalem auction house last year. I finally understand why some say Einstein was disappointed by the essence of science and resorted to philosophy in his later years. I mean, look at the words, how cliched and yet powerful!

【P2】事件类：不寻常的一餐

Describe an unforgettable dinner/an unusual meal

  • When the dinner was
  • Where the dinner was
  • Who you dined with
  • And explain why it is unforgettable/unusual
  1. 本题的A是an meal/dinner，描述一餐；B是unforgettable/unusual, 限制特征。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 讲述这一餐的时间地点人物等基本信息，引出难忘、不寻常的特征
    • 详述这一餐的过程
    • 总结这一餐难忘、不寻常的点，升华
  3. 时态建议：过去时，没商量。

  4. 其他：既然是一餐，那免不了要描述吃了什么、聊了什么、做了什么。一个饭局，不能只是吃吃喝喝，还要聊天谈心，甚至还要勾心斗角。把饭局的层次讲出来。

So I was invited to my girlfriend’s apartment for dinner last month, yay! Me and my girlfriend have been dating for 3 years, and that very dinner was unforgettably critical. I was so determined to win over her dad with a finely-tuned display of charm, decorum,machismo and wit. Well, I knew it was not gonna happen, but I got a chance to redeem myself anyway. I got everything ready. I put in the extra time to get that haircut, shaved and pressed my shirt and here we go.

I was welcomed by some sniffing of their Labrador and a slippery carpet, but everything went just fine. Greetings, smiles, firm handshakes. Yeah, that was great. Her mom cooked steamed fish, lovely dim sum and mixed veggies. I complimented on the dishes, sat beside their daughter, and began bonding with them.

We were talking about the music of their choice, some piano pieces of Debussy. I spurted out something about how Impressionism being in-between, then I realized something was wrong before I was reminded by a wink that her dad was an adamant fan of classical music, esp. of French Impressionism, something my girlfriend had told me the previous day. I soon added that Debussy and Ravel were precursors for modern era and things like that. Then her dad just smiled and told me not to worry about it and try less hard to bow and scrape.

I literally breathed a sigh of relief and thought at least we were both classical music buffs and there would be nothing wrong about that.

【每日表达】句型、习语、小幽默

wear someone down

burn someone out

finish someone off

knock yourself out

  • 每天的工作和学习让人劳累不堪。为了自己和家人的未来，拼一拼是值得的。但太大的压力、太多的工作量会让人疲惫不堪、喘不过气来。这四个词组都可以表示这个意思。注意最后一组knock后面只能加反身代词，而其他三组都可以加任意表示人的名词或代词。学习压力大，大家保重身体哦。
  • Stop doing your job like that, or you’ll knock yourself out!
  • With such heavy workloads, I almost burn myself out.
  • Three days of hard work has really finished him off.
  • All the stress and extra travel is beginning to wear him down.

All work and no play makes Jack a dull boy.

  • 这是一句非常好用的谚语，用来说明持续工作没有娱乐的后果。一直工作，没有玩耍，再聪明的人（比如某个Jack）也会变无趣。在使用的时候可以把Jack替换成自己的名字，就会有一种幽默的效果。
  • I really need to get out more often. You know what they say about all work and no play makes Lang a dull boy?

【综合技巧】追加评论与感受，非限定从就足够

  • Young people often shop on Taobao, which is a e-commerce website that has millions of transactions every day. 限制性定语从句
  • In the presence of so many people he was a little tense, which was understandable. 非限制性定语从句
  • Many Chinese people are fond of and are good at playing table tennis, which is quite natural for we win basically all the champions there are every year.
  • My mom claims to be allergic to cats’ fur, which I don’t know whether she’s messing with me or not.
  • My geography teacher was really patient to us, which… I think all teachers should be.
  • I currently get up at 7, do my morning routine exercises, skim the newspapers, feed my cat and go to work. Which was totally unimaginable only 1 year ago when I was still in college.

【纠音跟读】失去爆破1

  • The boy in a re(d) coa(t) was on a bla(ck) bi(ke) jus(t) now
  • An ol(d) lady in the grea(t) bi(g) world
  • What would you like, ho(t) tea or bla(ck) coffee?
  • I bought a chea(p) book, but it’s a goo(d) boo(k) too.
  • Goo(d) morning!
  • Goo(d) luck!

  • I wen(t) there alone a(t) nine las(t) night.

A most curious and useful thing to realize is that one never knows the impression one is creating on other people. One may often guess pretty accurately whether it is good, bad, or indifferent — some people render it unnecessary for one to guess, they practically inform one — but that is not what I mean. I mean much more than that. I mean that one has one’s self no mental picture corresponding to the mental picture which one’s personality leaves in the minds of one’s friends. Has it ever struck you that there is a mysterious individual going around, walking the streets, calling at houses for tea, chatting, laughing, grumbling, arguing, and that all your friends know him and have long since added him up and come to a definite conclusion about him — without saying more than a chance, cautious word to you; and that that person is you? Supposing that you came into a drawing room where you were having tea, do you think you would recognize yourself as an individuality? I think not. You would be apt to say to yourself as guests do when disturbed in drawing rooms by other guests: “Who’s this chap? Seems rather queer. I hope he won’t be a bore.” And yourfirst telling would be slightly hostile. Why, even when you meet yourself in an unsuspected mirror in the very clothes that you have put on that very day and that you know by heart, you are almost always shocked by the realization that you are you. And now and then, when you have gone to the glass to arrange your hair in the full sobriety of early morning, have you not looked on an absolute stranger, and has not that stranger piqued your curiosity? And if it is thus with precise external details of form, colour, and movement, what may it not be with the vague complex effect of the mental and moral individuality?
