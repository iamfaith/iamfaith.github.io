Day 10 Speaking

Posted on 2018-08-04

【P1】人物类：名人

一定注意对名气名声的利弊讨论、对名人的态度、名人的社会责任等。

Who is your favorite celebrity in your country?

My favorite celebrity has gotta be Jin Xing, the most illustrious杰出的 ballerina and talk show hostess in China. She was one of the first few transgender women in China to receive the government’s approval to undergo a sex change. I mean, in the 1990s. That alone is inspiring, right?

In general, how do people become famous?

In this information age, people become famous in all sorts of ways. Traditional path is still available. You become famous when you have some special knacks for singing, dancing, performing, and you find the perfect platform on which you shine. Then, don’t be surprised when you hit the stardom after simply uploading some hilarious videos of you and your friends having fun.

Do you think, to be famous, a person needs to have some special talent or abilities?

Well, you still remember the good old days when people needed special talent to get famous, right? Well, they’re gone. In this information age, many people mysteriously become famous by all the hype overnight.

Would you want to be a celebrity?

Nah. I mean, what if I change my mind and wanna lead an ascetic苦行僧 life when I turn 40? Plus, I don’t want my life to be pried too much by paparazzi or inquisitive people.

【P2】事件类：街市购物

Describe a time that you were shopping in a street market

  • When it was
  • Where you were
  • What you bought
  • How you felt about the shopping experience
  1. 本题的A是a time，描述经历；B是you were shopping in a street market, 限制内容。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 可选择直接用时间顺序讲故事，时间地点、开端、起因
    • 详述购物的过程，注意不要变流水账，抓住特征，比如购物的心得、砍价经历、战利品等
    • 简述感受，发表感叹，适度升华
  3. 时态建议：讲故事用过去时，述及购物对现在的影响时可用现在完成时。描述街市基本情况应用一般现在时。
  4. 其他：本题比较单纯可爱，用时间顺序讲一次购物经历即可。注意不要讲成流水账，突出一两个购物中的亮点，比如心得、砍价、战利品、心情变化等。

Well, first of all, I love street markets. They have been centers of commerce for centuries. Even in the age of Walmart and Amazon, these casual retail spaces, usually populated by small, independent vendors, are thriving in tons of parts of the world and are being revisited as viable places to shop in others.

My experience of eating shopping in Shilin Night Market of Taipei is superb. Shilin Night Market is the largest of Taipei’s famous night markets. It is best known for its gigantic food court. Independent vendors sell their specialties in a virtual feeding frenzy, and many locals and visitors consider this one of the best places to eat in all of Taiwan. I was dazzled by neon lights the minute I walked in. grilled squids, fried chicken, pigs blood cakes and other snack stalls were on both sides in order. Trust me, there’s a long queue in front of every single one of them, making the narrow street even more cramped. Let alone the lively bustle and hustle of pedestrians chewing various foods they’d bought and sauntered漫步 along. I soon found myself holding the renowned fried chicken in my right hand and fries in my left.

The best part of it was how I bought a beautiful shirt in one of the stores there with 50% off. So I entered this fashion store and soon couldn’t take my eyes off a red flannel shirt. The owner approached with a smile introducing how they got it with effort from Korea and how there was only one piece of its kind. I asked him to knock a few bucks off before he said a firm no. then I told him that I came from the mainland, which turned out to be a good strategy, since he smiled even more broadly and said yes immediately.

I don’t haggle／bargain a lot, but that felt awesome, to be honest.

【P2】事件类：不得不礼貌

Describe a situation where you had to be polite

  • What it is
  • What you did to be polite
  • Who were there
  • Why you had to be polite
  • And explain how you felt about it afterwards
  1. 本题的A是a situation，描述事件；B是where you had to be polite，限制事件内容。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 先介绍该事件的基本情况（时间、地点、人物），引出需礼貌的原因
    • 按时间顺序讲述事件，要有细节有内心活动
    • 总结事件带来的感受
  3. 时态建议：描述事件基本信息时用一般现在时，紧跟着讲故事用过去时，故事中涉及现在仍未改变的规律性动作时用一般现在时。内心活动及人物对话若用直接引语，用一般现在时。总结感受时用过去时、一般现在时、现在完成时。
  4. 其他：也可以略过提纲a)，直接开门见山讲故事，注意全程用过去时串起来。讲故事讲究细节，所以不要忽略对人物内心活动、动作神态等的描写。不描写事件，只叙述经过也符合题意，但注定得不到高分。另外，讲故事尽量少用直接引语，不符合考官对“即兴”的认知，改为间接引语更合适。

I will describe a miserable experience in a supposedly pleasant dinner. It was a rather formal one to which my dad was invited to conduct his business at work, about his new construction project or something. It has nothing to do with me, right? Well, that’s what I was thinking, until he dragged me to it and asked me a favor. Traditionally, in a Chinese dinner, people urge others to drink, esp. when talking about business. To me It’s never a good idea to consume alcohol during a business meal. The purpose of the meeting is to conduct business, and at the very least alcohol will slow down or cloud the thought process. And my dad needed me to help him decline possible requests.

I got this, I thought to myself. But I was wrong. That dinner was a total debacle崩溃. Despite the fact that I despise social etiquette礼仪, I tried my best to be polite from initial greetings, handshakes, to the end when dad and I lurched out drunk. My smile gradually dwindled away as more and more beer and baijiu were served. They just paid no heed to my warning about how my dad would get a buzz after like only two glasses of wine and made him drink incredibly much. To add insult to injury, they made me drink too. I was fighting back the urge to throw a tantrum and stand up for my dad when he sneakily put an arm on my legs telling me not to.

What the heck was wrong with those people? I complained it to my dad the next day when we were both sober. And my dad gave me a grin and told me that this was adult life, and it sucks, but I had to acclimate myself to it. What a bummer!

【P2】事件类：参加派对

Describe a party that you joined

  • When it took place
  • Where it was
  • Whose party it was
  • What you did there
  • And how you felt about the party
  1. 本题的A是a party，描述经历；B是you joined, 限制内容。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 可选择直接用时间顺序讲故事，时间地点、开端、起因
    • 详述派对的过程，注意不要变流水账，抓住特征
    • 简述感受，发表感叹，适度升华
  3. 时态建议：讲故事用过去时，述及派对对现在的影响时可用现在完成时
  4. 其他：本题比较单纯可爱，用时间顺序讲一个派对就完了。注意不要讲成流水账，把点变少、变大。

I’d like to tell you about a special birthday party I joined this March. So my best friend Jerry who was my roommate back in college called me the other day inviting me to sleep over. I was like, what am I, 12? He laughed and said it would be a blackout sleepover party for his 24th birthday. I thought it was a code for pajamas. Then I wore one, took my present for him and went straight to his apartment.

Well, he meant it. I got there a little early and bumped into his clumsiness of preparations. So I gave him a hand, moved all the furniture out of the way and threw some blankets and pillows on the ground. 20 minutes later, people came, scrambling to sit near Jerry. Alright, so he’s apparently a little bit more popular than I am. We turned off all the lights, computers and TV, and lit up the room using candles. We sat together in a circle, played some music, enjoying drinks and snacks with interesting conversations, fun games and a healthy dose of flirting. It really brought me back to a simpler time, that carefree畅快 childhood which helped minimize stress and allowed me to just laugh and have a great time without worrying about anything.

The honesty in the air made it possible for everyone to confess, of our own volition意志. To our surprise, Eric, the other roommate of ours gave an affectionate monologue in the dark and admitted that he had the hots for Amy, our beautiful monitor back then. Which made us so excited, ecstatic欣喜若狂 actually because we all knew his secret crush all the way through.

Guess what? I really crashed on his couch that night, cause I was hopelessly drunk.

【每日表达】句型、习语、小幽默

rude awakening

  • 有没有这样的经历？自己盲目自信能做好一件事，赢得一个比赛，赢得一个人，结果到见分晓的那一刻才幡然醒悟，原来自己得不到。这种幡然醒悟就叫rude awakening. 搭配have或be in for a rude awakening.
  • He’d thought this would be a cushy job, but then he was in for a rude awakening when he really did it.

a dime a dozen

  • 这个习语是我们中文讲的“烂大街”最好的对应，说一个东西、一种人、一个现象满街都是，一点都不稀奇。在十九世纪的美国，常见的日用品比如苹果鸡蛋牙刷，它们都是a dime a dozen，用一个dime就能买一打。现在引申为比喻那些因为太多而价值不高的东西。
  • Unlike 20 years ago, American fast-food restaurants are a dime a dozen now.
  • The so-called experts are a dime a dozen.

If I understand the matter at all, science and art are the obverse and reverse of Nature’s medal; the one expressing the external order of things, in terms of feeling, the other in terms of thought.
