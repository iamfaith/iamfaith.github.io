【P1】想象类：（太空）旅行

Do you want to travel to the outer space?

I mean, duh. Who wouldn’t wanna have that once-in-a-lifetime experience, right? I would be ecstatic欣喜若狂的 to see the breathtaking milky way.

What would you do if you had that opportunity?

I would grasp it and make the best of it. I might figure out how to post a geo-tagged地理标志 tweet there. No one has done it before, right?

Do you think it’s necessary to see other planets?

I actually don’t. we’ve only got this one earth which we should protect and love. Rather than resorting to other habitable planets which we haven’t found yet, we might as well take good care of this one.

When was the last time you went traveling?

It was last summer when I went on a boat trip to Hainan, a tropical island in the South of China. I got to see the breathtaking sunrise on the deck of the cruise ship. it was such a once-in-a-lifetime experience, wasn’t it?

Do you like to travel by air?

Not really. The greatest dilemma in the world is between comfy but costly first class and affordable but narrow economy. Not the case were you a millionaire, of course.

【P2】事件类：特殊衣服

Describe a time you wore a type of clothes for a special occasion (describe something you wear in a special occasion)

  • What the occasion was
  • What you wore
  • Where you bought the clothes
  • And explain what other people at the occasion thought of your clothes
  1. 本题的A是a time，描述经历；B是you wore a type of clothes for a special occasion, 限制内容。实质上是经历+物件类题目
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 可选择直接用时间顺序讲故事，时间地点、开端、起因
    • 详述穿这件衣服的场合、过程，描述这件衣服
    • 简述感受、他人评价，发表感叹，适度升华
  3. 时态建议：讲故事用过去时，描述衣服本身可选用一般现在时。最后述及感受和影响可用现在完成时。
  4. 其他：本题的另一版本在括号中，注意这个版本的时态为现在时，即这件衣服不能只在某个场所穿一次，而应该在特殊场合都穿。因此描述的时候不能只讲故事，还要把事件用现在时讲成时常发生的。

You know how nutty as a fruitcake we used to be when we just entered college and finally got rid of our folks who kept pestering纠缠 us on our looks and outfits, right? Well,turned out I went completely overboard the first day in college. I joined a rock music club, the only one of its kind in my school back then and I was invited to the welcome party. Well, of course I needed to spruce myself up a little bit to impress people. And that was why I bought this black leather jacket in the nearby mall with rivets on the shoulder. It’s got this fully lined interior with one snap-closed pocket and one zippered pocket. A rock star, I murmured to myself in front of the mirror. I got this.

Then I strutted through the door of the bar wearing a crooked cocky smile with my hands in pockets. And that slim-fit jacket glittered. I thought I was the spotlight that night. But things soon went wrong. Instead of getting supposed cheers from the crowd, I was scalded by weird looks and whispers. The performers shouting Linkin Park on stage were staring at me moving closer. And the cute guitarist even winked at me. That’s right, I felt like a rock star now.

After the performances, I felt nervous to mingle with people I didn’t know, but I did it anyway. Most of freshmen were kind enough not to comment on my jacket but their occasional glances sold them out. That was when I decided to take it off with a sigh and fit in better.

【P2】事件类：朋友做过你没做

Describe something interesting that your friend has done but you haven’t

  • What it was
  • Where your friend did it
  • When your friend did it
  • And explain why it was interesting
  1. 本题的A是something，描述事物；B是your friends has done but you haven’t, 限制内容。本题为事物+事件的结合。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 介绍这件事物及其特征
    • 详述朋友参加的经历、有趣的地方
    • 简述感受，发表感叹，适度升华
  3. 时态建议：介绍事物本身可用一般现在时，介绍朋友经历时用过去时，转而说为何有趣时可用一般现在时。
  4. 其他：此题要求的事件经历描述方式是转述，一定注意要时不时安插间接引语标志，提醒考官你仍在转述，而不要用直接引语。

Have you ever heard of hashtag YOLO, an acronym for you only live once which is a call to live life to its fullest extent, even embracing behavior which carries inherent risk? Well my friend Jimmy is a believer. He’s an amateur extreme sports athlete who hasbeen through a lot in life. Trust me, there were cases in which he got on the brink ofdeath and then survived.

So I was not at all surprised when he told me he’d tried hang gliding the other day. He used the stimulating word defying gravity when talking about the sport and his experience. He told me that it’s beyond imagination to harness the power of nature to soar miles across the open countryside, at heights of up to 1,000 meters and speeds of over 20 mph with a hang glider. It’s like using the same naturally occurring currents of air that birds use to fly, and you can take to the skies and enjoy this exhilarating experience, no matter what your age, physical ability or background. He joined a gliding club in Shanghai and had his first try-out 3 days later. He said it felt like heaven flying in the sky. This kind of floating felt exactly like a dream too, one where he was flying above treetops, getting a glimpse of how beautiful life can be. Anytime he got a ripple of wind it was like soaring up in a giant swing.

Well of course I missed the technical part cause it was full of jargons that I could never remember, but I so lusted after it hearing his descriptions alone. I’ve never thought about trying any extreme sport myself, for when it comes to sports, it’s always safe better than sorry, right? But maybe in 5 years I will change. I don’t know.

【P2】事件类：帮助专心

Describe something you do to keep yourself concentrated in work/study.

  • What it is
  • How often you do it
  • When you started doing it
  • And explain why it helped yourself concentrate
  1. 本题的A是something，描述事物；B是you do to keep yourself concentrated in work/study, 限制内容。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 介绍这件事物及其特征
    • 详述使用经过、时间、好处
    • 简述感受，发表感叹，适度升华
  3. 时态建议：介绍事物本身可用一般现在时，介绍经历时用过去时，转而说为何有用可用一般现在时。
  4. 其他：任何地方的题库tips可能都不完全准确。此题题干为现在时，但tips最后一条为过去时，应为谬误。如果真遇到此情况，那么可以只说描述中故事那一次给自己带来的帮助。

I’d like to describe a simple but rather cool productivity method, the Pomodoro Technique, aka tomato method, invented by some Italian, which helps one power through distractions, hyper-focus, and get things done in short bursts, while taking frequent breaks to come up for air and relax. Interestingly, this method got its name after a tomato timer the inventor used as a college student.

Here’s how it works. To implement it, all you’ll need is a timer. Beyond that, there are no special apps, books, or tools required. Choose a task to be accomplished,

set the timer to 25 minutes, work on the task until the timer rings, take a short break for like 5 minutes, and after 4 intervals take a longer break. That’s it. Pomodoro is a cyclical system. You work in short sprints, which makes sure you’re consistently productive. You also get to take regular breaks that bolster your motivation and keep you creative.

Concentration had been so hard for me until I read about this technique 6 years ago and soon realized that it wasn’t my fault. Turned out it’s against human nature to focus on a certain task for more than 25 minutes, right? So back then I was working on my new book and the last half proved a lot easier when I resorted to this magical method.

What’s more interesting about it is that there actually is a website tomato-timer.com on which you have this set timer for 25 minutes. Push the start, stop and reset button just like any other timer and you get it! Not surprisingly, there’s this Youtuber who shared her 2 hours of study process using this method and apparently 1m viewers follow suit. It’s a cult, isn’t it?

【P3技巧】讲故事与讲道理

Story-Telling (concrete examples, life experiences, historical events, imaginations, jokes…)

Vividness is the key

Appropriate tense and mood (did, do, have done, will do, would do…)

Chronological (when, then…)

Practice!

Reasoning (abstract examples, reasons, ideas, speculations, comparisons…)

Logic is the key

Introduction + Body (+Conclusion)

Paralleling, Comparison and Analogy

Support: Phenomena, Reports, Research and data, Personal experiences, Stories of celebrities/historical figures…

What is the difference of traveling with your family members and traveling alone?

下面我提供两种路线的答案。看看他们有什么不同哦

ST: Traveling with your family members is just so much fun, esp. for a family person myself. I can’t live without them! Just like last September when I went on a ferry trip with my family. I couldn’t forget the breathtaking sunrise we appreciated on the first day together on the deck. We were standing abreast, smiling and mimicking seagull’s cry. And we took a selfie of us and those ivory birds. If I had gone there on my own, I would’ve felt so lonely. I mean, who would be there to make fun of my crack of voice?

R: Traveling alone is my cup of tea and something I do at least one time a year. Firstly, you get to carry out your own plan or itinerary and don’t have to compromise. I mean, they’re your family, what if they’re not happy to go to the Met but prefer to picnic in the Central Park? Just be ready to give up your paintings and sculptures. Secondly, traveling alone, you can alter your plan whenever you want. When you don’t feel like going out someday in a journey, just indulge yourself a stay-in at the hotel. Finally, some may say that you will be lonely traveling alone. Well, you may be alone, but you can’t be lonely. Come on, this is the so-called information age, and you can always post the 10-s video of breathtaking sunrise on your Moments and get as many likes as you want, right?

【每日表达】句型、习语、小幽默

better safe than sorry

极限运动敢玩吗？对小部分富有冒险精神的人来说，当然敢。人生就是拿来冒险的，毕竟不去好好体验人生怎么算活过一回呢？但我相信对大部分人来说，还是安安全全的比较重要。那么，我们可以用better safe than sorry来形容这种心理状态。宁愿不那么有趣好玩，宁愿抱憾，也不去冒险，省得出事。

When it comes to sports, it’s always better safe than sorry, right?

not/never in a million years

Hyperbole（夸张）是英文最常见的修辞手法之一，有很多表达法都极其夸张、不符实际，但很好地表达出其中包含的实意。比如not/never in a million years，讲某个事情不可能发生，讲说它一百万年都不可能。这也是常见的表示拒绝和表示否定的表达法。这在英美人士的口语常见句型中起码能排进前50，然而咱却说得很少。学起来吧！

  • when will you consider marrying me?
  • Never in a million years.

I prefer to study in the morning when most people at my age are still in their sweet dreams in the morning, right? I get to breathe in fresh and brisk air and enjoy mild temperature. besides, there are not so many distractions in the morning so that I canconcentrate harder and be more productive.
