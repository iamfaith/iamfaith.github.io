Day 18 Speaking

Posted on 2018-08-12

【P1】人物类：家人

How often do you meet your family?

I only get to meet my family once a year, in Spring Festival when all Chinese people are supposed to go back home and have a family reunion.

Do you want to live with your family in the future?

Not really, since I have this independent streak in me fostered by my family. I would miss them and visit them quite often though.

Are you close to all of your family members?

I grow up in this nuclear family where we take care of each other all the time. So, I guess, yeah, I’m close to both of my parents.

How has your family influenced you?

I’ve got a wry sense of humor, and sometimes apparently inappropriate. Both my parents are hilarious, and you always know what’s in store for you during a meal – my dad’s remarkably funny jokes.

【P1】交通类：船

Do you like boats?

Not really, my negligible experiences of being on a boat could be better. Basically, I suffered from seasickness big time.

Would you like to have your own boat?

Nah, I think I’ll pass, even if I turned a millionaire tomorrow. Being on a boat is likepurgatory for me.

Is boat trip popular in your country?

I don’t think so. Boat trips are extravagant for most people, well, maybe not for the wealthy community. I’d rather rent a boat and have a good time with my friends on one of those artificial lakes in a nearby park.

【P1】基本信息：学习与工作

Do you study or work?

Currently I’m a university student majoring in… and I’m in my junior year now.

I work as an actuary in an insurance company and my job is to tell my company how much they should charge people for insurance based on risks, mortality, accident, sickness, disability, and retirement rates, etc.

Why did you choose to do that type of work?

For your information, I majored in mathematics back in university but my advisors told me that theoretical things are not for me, and that was why I turned to actuarial industry after graduation, thinking that only a certain amount of math skills were needed.

Do you like your job?

Not really. Turned out this job is so much more tedious than I previously thought. I had to deal with tons of paperwork and stuff. I’m considering quitting but not ready for it.

Do you miss being a student?

I miss being a student, like, big time. Do you still remember the good old days in school where you spent carefree days studying and hanging with your mates? How I wish I could be back there.

What’s your major? What subjects are you studying?

I’m a law major and I need to learn how a legislature, an executive and a judiciary work separately and together, along with our constitution, civil law, criminal law and so forth.

Why did you choose to study that subject?

I had been obsessed with Boston Legal, an American legal comedy-drama television series before I entered college. And I really long to be a lawyer for years to come.

Are there many students in China majoring in that?

Let’s be modest here and I would say there are half of a million law majors, give or take. You can basically see a law school in every single university or college.

Are you looking forward to working?

Absolutely. More than anything else. I want to earn some good money to support my family and fulfill my ambition.

【P2】物件类：重要水域

Describe an important river/lake (/area of water) in your country

  • What this river is called
  • Where it is
  • How you knew about it
  • How long/big it is
  • And explain why it is important
  1. 本题的A是a river/lake (water)，描述水域；B是important, in your country，限制属性和地点。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 描述此水域的基本信息
    • 详述此水域重要的原因
    • 对此水域的感受，升华
  3. 时态建议：通篇一般现在时，讲自己第一次见到或知道的经历用过去时
  4. 其他：雅思口语P2关于水域的问题可以说是自成体系了，出题者显然特别喜欢江河湖海，几乎每个题季都有一道以上跟水有关的题目。这是典型说明文，应当把某个水域的特征详细进行介绍，落脚点是“重要”。注意，重要，不是美丽。

This is a no-brainer for me. I’d like to describe one of the most beautiful and fairly important lakes in China, Qinghai Lake, which is the excellent picturesque scene of blue lake, golden rape blossom, and mountains in the distance. It’s also the largest inland saltwater in China, a paradise for avid bird-watchers and of paramount importance in terms of biodiversity.

Qinghai Lake is located in northeast Qinghai Tibet Plateau and northwest Xining City, Qinghai Province, surrounded by four high mountains. The best travel season falls on the autumn months when the vast prairie wear a golden coat of rape flowers, refreshing fragrance drifts with the wind, dotted herdsman tents look like stars in the sky, herds moves as clouds, charming sunrise and sunset consisting of a poetic painting.

I call it important because of one tiny island called Bird Island, a bird refuge which plays a vital part in providing a transitory home to countless numbers of migratory birds which arrive there from Southern China and Southeast Asia. Such birds include the spotted head wild goose, brown head wild goose, fish gull, red sheldrake, cormorants, and black-neck crane. There are more than 100,000 birds that inhabit this small island to nest, lay eggs, hatch, and raise their young. For the avid bird-watcher, Bird Island is certainly a paradise, but even the inexperienced bird watcher cannot help being impressed with thesheer numbers of creatures that gather here. Their constant buzz of activity and theecho of their mating calls, bickering over territory, and shrill cries of their hungry offspring connect the listener with the wonders of the natural world.

This very lake is the perfect epitome of biodiversity, for the local government spares no effort to preserve indigenous and exotic birds, antelopes and fishes. Various campaigns have been conducted to disseminate the idea.

Unfortunately, the Bird Island has been closed indefinitely in order to better protect the fragile ecological system there.

第18天【P2】物件类：城市发展

Describe a recent development (such as new roads, shopping centers) in your city

  • What this development is
  • How it could help people
  • What benefits it could bring
  • Where you saw it
  • And explain whether it changes your life or not
  • and explain how you felt about it)

  1. 本题的A是a development，描述城市一处发展成果；B是recent, in your city, 限制时间地点。

  2. 提纲建议：

    • 描述此发展成果的基本信息（时间地点、功能性等）
    • 详述此发展成果对你及当地人的好处
    • 对此发展成果的评价和感受

  3. 时态建议：介绍这个发展成果用一般现在时，描述建成过程或经历用过去时。

  4. 其他：development有一词多义，就题目括号中给的例子来看，指的是建筑和设施类的具体发展成果，那么在选材时要尽量避免比较虚的发展成果（比如空气变好了、人们生活水平提高了等）。

My city is a rather tiny one, and it surely is a surprise that new development is occurring at a breakneck pace there, and while much of it is happening on the individual level, anot-inconsequential portion of it is also lumped into megaprojects rising all over the 4 districts. Fortunately, one of them has finished recently. The riverside center. Well, part of it.

There are several parts to the West River District megaproject known as Riverside Center, an eight-acre development rising on an old industrial lot and exposed railyard between two main streets on the west part waterfront. The project, conceived by some unknown developers, has been pocked by uncertainty from the get-go, but one of the planned 3 buildings has completed – the biggest shopping mall in town, with the other two under construction which are supposed to be the new administrative center and a skyscraper office building.

This 55-story shopping mall could be the tallest of its kind in the southwest of China. If it wasn’t for the top floor, this mall would be no different than any of its counterpart.

Half area of the top floor was mapped out a municipal museum with the log of city development as well as antiques discovered in the city. Which hasn’t opened yet. And the other half, a perfect observation place where one gets to enjoy a panorama of the whole city.

And yet the most exciting part is that residents can now enjoy strolling in the newly-built park that connects the three buildings. It feels kinda surreal to be in the center with three eerily tall buildings forming a triangle. But it’s also an amazing garden for thousand employees working in the three edifice to wind down.

【P2】物件类：科技产品

Describe a piece of technology you like using except computers

  • What this technology is
  • When it was invented
  • How you learned about it
  • How often you use it
  • And explain what difference it brings to your life
  1. 本题的A是a technology，描述一项科技产品；B是you like using except computers, 限制你对这项产品的情感倾向。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 简述此科技产品的基本信息、发明时间、基本使用方法等
    • 详述你使用此产品的方式、心得或故事
    • 发表评价和感受
  3. 时态建议：介绍这项产品用一般现在时，如果涉及到发明或你与其互动的故事，用过去时。
  4. 其他：注意，这里的a piece of technology指的是一件带高科技的产品，注意，产品，不是某项高科技本身。为了让大家明确这一点，还专门贴心地附上了except computers。为了让串题不明显，尽量也避开手机。这道题看似是说明文，但题目后半句说到自己喜欢用，那么也可以用故事（记叙文）来展开。

I’d like to describe a gizmo that helps me sleep, a sleep aid device thing I’ll call it. I put this little guy on my nightstand the size of a football, beside my bonsai which is also said to be conducive to my sleep. It produces the so-called white noise, a rather constant and steady sound that claims to make one fall asleep faster and their sleep deeper.

Apparently, I’m super sound-sensitive, and even bothered by the slightest sound. The dog next door barking, someone nearby beeping their horn, my partner snoring like a chainsaw, and I’m often staring at the ceiling in frustration rather than sleeping soundly.

As I mentioned, this handy device isn’t intended to play so loudly that they overwhelmoutside disruptions. Instead, it produces white noise and works with my brain’s natural tendency to mostly notice new or variable sounds while tuning out steady, repetitiousnoise. That’s why people who live near freeways or in big cities tend to “get used to” steady traffic sounds, yet awaken when a taxi blares its horn, a helicopter flies overhead, or a dog suddenly barks.

I love this gadget with its soft, steady, repeating loops of sound, helping mask sudden or unusual noise, thus lulling my brain into relaxation, and eventually, sweet sweet sleep. Of course, really loud or irregular noise, such as my baby’s crying, is still going to break through, but in general, I sleep far more soundly with white noise playing in the background than without.

Well I have no idea who the inventor is, but whoever that is is a true hero. Thank god this little guy is right there with me. Sometimes I don’t turn it on, and just the thought that it is there soothes me.

【每日表达】句型、习语、小幽默

on fire

  • on a roll
  • 是不是有时候感觉自己是随性型选手，成绩好坏全看发挥。发挥好的时候所向披靡，发挥不好的时候萎靡不振。这种发挥好的时候的感觉，就可以用on fire或on a roll来形容，指最棒的状态，成功的状态。On a roll还可以形容因为状态好而变得话非常多，在兴头上；on fire还可以形容正处在情绪非常强烈的时刻。
  • With a growing economy and a dropping crime rate, the city has been on a roll.
  • He’s a funny guy, and last night he was really on a roll.
  • The team has been on fire, winning 10 of its last 11 games.
  • He was on fire with enthusiasm when his dad tried to teach him to hunt.

follow/steer/take the middle course/way/path

  • 有些人常常走极端，不左就右。然而很多情况下，极端情形都是比较危险的，会给自己和他人带来伤害。更多的人愿意在做选择时尽量往中间靠，这样最不可能发生危险。这与我们古人中庸的做法有类似。取折中的做法、走中间道路避免危害的行为就叫follow/steer/take the middle course/way/path. 注意哦，排列组合起来可以有9种说法。
  • Most parents steer a middle course between strict discipline and letting their kids run wild.

【P3技巧】比较类与利弊类

比较类 Compare similarities and differences

  • 可细分与不可细分
  • 横向与纵向
  • What are the (similarities and) differences between A and B?
  • Please compare A and B.
  • How has … changed in… years?
  • Do you prefer A or B, why?
  • A and B, which is better?
  • What are some differences between pop stars now and those in the past, like 30 years ago?
  • Which do you think is better? Raising children by parents themselves or by their grandparents?
  • Is there any difference between technology in cities and in small towns?
  • What is the difference between the kind of joy that money can buy and the kind that money can’t?
  • What is the difference between learning history from books and from the Internet?

利弊类 Illustrating advantages and disadvantages

  • 用假想敌帮助思考，注意单复数
  • Can you think of anyadvantages/disadvantages of…?
  • What are the advantages and disadvantagesof…?
  • What are the benefits of…?
  • Are there any disadvantages of…?
  • Would you prefer to …, and why?
  • What are the advantages and disadvantages of the popularity of talent shows on TV?
  • Are there any disadvantages of preserving old buildings in modern cities?
  • What are the benefits of letting students choose the subjects that they learn?
  • Would you prefer to go straight to your destination while traveling? Why?
  • Can you think of any advantages and disadvantages of working in a foreign country?

今日任务：回答下列问题，每题不低于40秒：

  1. What are some differences between pop stars now and those in the past, like 30 years ago?
  2. What is the difference between the kind of joy that money can buy and the kind that money can’t?
  3. What are the advantages and disadvantages of the popularity of talent shows on TV?
  4. What are the benefits of letting students choose the subjects that they learn?

【纠音跟读】语调综合

One way my personality has changed↑ since I was a child↑ is that I became honest. When I was a little kid↑, I always lied to my parents↑ because I didn’t want them to know that I stole money from them↑, didn’t do homework well↑ and played video games outside. Once I stole five Yuan from my mom↑ and used the money to play video games. Also↑, I was late for school. On that day↑, I lied to my teacher↑ and my parents. Even if I wasn’t caught by anyone↑, I still felt guilty of lying. I found that one lie makes many↑ and that once people realized that I had lied to them↑, they started not to believe me. I felt sad about myself at that time. Gradually↑, I started to hold the belief that honesty↑ is the best policy.

千年

In an age when human beings have learnt the code of human life, and can transmit their knowledge in seconds from one continent to another, no mother in the world can understand why her child should be left to die of malnutrition or preventable disease. No one can understand why they should be driven from their home, or imprisoned or tortured for expressing their beliefs. No one can understand why the soil their parents tilled has turned to desert, or why their skills have become useless and their family is left hungry. People know that these challenges cannot be met by one country alone, or by one government alone. Change cannot be held back by frontiers. Human progress has always come from individual and local initiatives, freely devised and then freely adapted elsewhere.
