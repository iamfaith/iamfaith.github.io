【P1】日常类：礼物

本题为日常类话题，问题集中在送礼物的场合、礼物类型价格、对礼物的态度等。注意积累自己送和收的礼物及常见礼物类型。

What kind of gifts do you prefer to receive? Why?

As a stationery buff, an extravagant diamond fountain pen would be nice. Thank you very much. I was kidding. I would feel understood if I received a regular but durable pen since I’ve been practicing calligraphy and the repairer botched my last one.

Do you like to send gifts to others?

I’m afraid not. Buying gifts for someone else never ranked as one of my top 10 most fun things to do. So unless it’s on some specially important occasions and for people I really care about, I’d rather not squander my time being excessively considerate.

Do you like to send expensive gifts?

Certainly not. I mean, sending expensive gifts means the recipient would better send back something even more expensive. And that would definitely add up. By the way, in China, receiving costly gifts without returning one is considered a major social faux pas. The price is still a bit over my budget

What kinds of gifts are popular in your country?

I suppose fine teas, fruit baskets and regional specialty foods are my top three here. Fine teas are a traditionally lavish gifts and some brands can be really expensive. Xinjiang nuts and dried fruits or any other treats from your hometown as gifts show your sincerity. Besides, you can never go wrong to send fruit baskets when visiting people.

Why do people send gifts?

Cause we care. About the wellbeing of our friends & family. You never give them gifts on their birthdays, anniversaries or any other special occasions involuntarily, don’t you?

  • 时态建议：描述人物特征、状态、走红程度、评价等均用一般现在时，若描述其做过的事、奋斗史等用过去时。
  1. 其他：注意本题的B，一定要详述popular（表现、原因等），否则算跑题。

【P2】人物类：受欢迎人物

Describe a popular person (you know)
You should say:
Who this person is
What kind of person he/she is
Why you think this person is popular
How you got to know him/her
How this person influences the public
And explain how you feel about this person

  1. 本题的A是a person，描述人；B是popular，限制属性。
  2. 人物基本信息、外貌性格特征、引出popular
  3. 互动（认识的过程等）、popular的表现、如何影响大众
  4. 评价与升华
  5. 时态建议：以一般现在时为主即可，认识的过程用过去时，可辅之以完成时作为加分点。
  6. 其他：题目比较宽泛，自由度较高。与喜剧演员完美匹配。串题时注意不要出现其他题目的特征词。

I’m gonna describe Jane Zhang, a 34 year-old Chinese pop singer, who is arguably the most popular female singer born in mainland China. She is the first Chinese artist to sing on the Vitoria’s Secret Fashion Show and the first singer to be invited to appear in the Oprah Winfrey Show. You may get a hint of how popular she is by noticing that she’ got more than 40 million followers on Weibo, the Chinese equivalent of Twitter.

I first knew about her when she took part in a singing competition, Super Voice Girl in 2005. Back then, she was a girl next door, a quiet, intimidated local pub singer in Chengdu, a survivor of divorced parents like me, who would stand on the stage, singing Mariah Carey and Tina Turner. I didn’t notice this angelic girl until she sang Loving You and belted that signature whistle register, which won her this affectionate nickname “Dolphin Princess”. Although she didn’t win, she was the one who shot the stardom and managed to land a recording contract which started her incredible singing career.

She is such a versatile singer-songwriter who is probably the first talent show contestant ever to insist on singing English songs all the way to the finale. I still remember how the judge panel back then suggested that she sing more Chinese songs to attract more public votes. Well, she just smiled to those comments, paid no heed to them and performed one classic after another. Admittedly, it was because of her that I, a junior high student back then, decided to learn English harder. And more interestingly, an astonishingly number of singers began singing English songs in talent shows.

She has remained my favorite Chinese pop artist and a respected role model until now. whenever I get lost in what I do, I’m gonna be like, hold on to it, just like Jane once did in her twenties.

【P2】人物类：童年基友

Describe a childhood friend of yours (Describe a time you spent with your childhood friend)

  • When you met him/her
  • Where you met him/her
  • What you did with your friend
  • How you felt about the friend
  • (Who this friend is
  • How you knew about each other
  • What you did together

  • And explain why you remembered it.)

    . A是a friend, 描述人；B是childhood + of yours, 限定范围。可以解读为人物类题目，也可以解读为人物+事件类。

  • 描述童年基友的基本情况、外貌性格特征，最大特点
  • 扩大最大特点，分析或小故事
  • 总结，拓展现状，适当升华
  1. 时态建议：描述人物特征时用一般现在时，讲故事用过去时，最后的评价如果涉及到类别问题，可用一般现在时；述及对现在的影响可用现在完成时。
  2. 其他：注意括号中的版本，以事件为主，可以略讲人物特征。

I remember there’s this bad pun in Friends going “The ship that never sinks is friendship.” I’ll never forget the day I met my best friend. I was in fourth grade back then, and it was in one of the most tedious science classes. Before it began, I immediately noticed we had a new classmate. She had blonde streaks in her hair and glitter under her eyes, and she was as smiley as can be. I thought to myself, I have to be her friend. I later learned her name was Kelly and it wasn’t long before she was coming over after school almost every day and I was claiming her as my best friend.

There was something about our personalities that just clicked. She was always bubbly and upbeat and had a way of instantly changing my mood on a bad day. She became a part of my family and I a part of hers — we were inseparable.

But, we have a major difference. I was like, a star in school back then, arguably the best singer our elementary school has to offer. She, on the other hand, knows nothing about music. So the time I tried to teach her to read sheet music and sing Danny Boy for our chorus competition is a funny one. Trust me, that was a hilarious disaster. Turned out she was completely tone-deaf! Finding the right pitch is purgatory for her. No matter how many times we tried, she just couldn’t sing do re mi right. Having said that, we still had a great time singing together. Picture the best singer singing with the worst, and you’ll know. Heavenly harmony, right?

Luckily, she still remains my best friend, and although we hang out less often than before, she takes up a special spot in my heart. And that’s one thing I wouldn’t trade.

【每日表达】句型、习语、小幽默

twiddle one’s thumbs
fidget with something
fiddle with (something)

人生就是有永远都开不完的会，领导在上面大放厥词，blablabla，你在台下坐着，思考等会儿要吃什么。领导没有要停的意思，于是你只好开始摆弄手机，又不能被HR看见，只好玩自己的手指。这种无聊、烦躁或紧张的时候开始摆弄什么东西，就可以用这三个表达法，非常生动。

  • After my Gaokao, I spent every day twiddling my thumbs and waiting for the postman.
  • That was the first time I met my girlfriend’s dad, and I was idly fidgeting with my phone before he came.
  • After getting my first watch, I was so excited that I fiddled with it and broke it.
Confucius says
Confucius he says

这是一个很棒的小幽默。你一定认为后面应该加一句论语里面的话对不对？那你就错了，后面可以加任何话，达成的效果就比较像“有位哲人曾经说过，少一点套路，多一点真诚。”这种感觉，对方会认为这个玩笑很可爱。不过还要看自己是不是这种人设哦。
