【P1】市政类：交通

本题为市政类话题，主要集中在城市公共交通建设、私家车现状、远距离轨道交通、飞机等。答题前可试着总结各交通工具的优缺点和异同，即可轻松应对。

Do you like travelling by train, by car or by air? Why?

Call me old fashioned, but, Travelling by train has my vote. I’m kind of claustrophobic幽闭恐惧症, and being trapped in the narrow space and seat of a cabin drives me crazy. Don’t get me started on how terrible it is to get carsick. Travelling by train, you get to hear the rumbling rhythm and enjoy the scenery outside.

What’s the most popular means of transportation in your hometown?

I suppose it’s gotta be private vehicles since the public transportation system’s a mess. And for a super tiny city like my hometown, traffic congestion is weirdly common. I guess that says something, doesn’t it?

How often do you travel by train?

When I’m traveling, the train is always my priority in terms of transportation. So like 3 or 4 times a year, give or take.

Do people in your city travel by taxi?

Well, some of them do commute by taxi, mostly when they can get a reimbursement for the travel expenses. but in rush hours, it’s never a good idea.

When was the last time you took a taxi?

It was like 2 hours ago when I took a taxi from my hotel to get here. And to be honest, I had been so nervous that I almost couldn’t fall asleep last night and got up a little bit late this morning. I had no choice.

Is traveling by train very popular in China?

You can say that. The high-speed rail is actually the best choice for a long-distance journey. It’s estimated that Chinese people will make nearly 400 million train trips over Chinese New Year.

Should everyone have their own car?

Well, not necessarily. Not everyone is suitable to drive. like me. I have such a bad sense of direction, and I will make a terrible driver. Besides, Driving should not be encouraged, considering rampant adj. (尤指疾病, 社会弊端)猖獗的; 无法控制的 air pollutions in the world. But of course, you can always turn to one of those electric cars. aggravate the problem of traffice congestion and the rampant air pollutions

Would you ride bikes to work in the future?

That’s exactly what I am doing on a daily basis. I ride to work with a cheerful mood esp. when seeing those vehicles stuck in the traffic. I will definitely keep doing it.

【P2】人物类：名人

Describe a famous person (that you are interested in) (in your country).
You should say:
Who this person is;
How you know this person;
How this person looks like;
What this person is famous for;
What sort of life they had before they became famous;
How this person became famous;
And explain why you like this person. (why he/she is important)
提示：

  1. 本题的A是a person，描述人；B是famous, (that you’re interested in, in your country)，限制属性（地点）。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 描述此人的基本信息，外貌、性格特征，引出famous
    • 详述成名过程、成名前故事等
    • 对ta的评价，为何喜欢，适当升华
  3. 时态建议：描述人物特征时用一般现在时，讲故事用过去时，最后的评价如果涉及到类别问题，可用一般现在时；述及对现在的影响可用现在完成时。
  4. 其他：说一位国内有名的人，即可涵盖所有题目版本

Ok, I’m gonna describe this highly controversial celebrity in China, Jin Xing, China’s most popular TV hostess, who has been many things in her life: dance prodigy, prima ballerina,decorated colonel in the People’s Liberation Army, choreographer, actress, wife and mother of three. She also has been a man.

She often appears on TV an elegant woman with artistic temperament with black curly hair and a long crooked nose. It’s actually hard to tell what she has been through. As early as 4 she felt she was different, not just in terms of her sexual identity but also thanks to a precocious talent for dance that would, by age 9, result in her admission to a prestigious troupe within the People’s Army. She not only was trained hard to be a ballerina, but became proficient with machine guns and learned how to place bombs delicately under bridges as well.

Jin went to New York to study modern dance, traveled and performed in Europe, and taught dance in Rome, followed by a world tour, and returned to China in her twenties. Then she underwent sex reassignment surgery after which her left leg was paralyzed for a while. Soon she established her own modern dance troupe, got married and adopted 3 sons. Her career as a TV personality skyrocketed thanks to regular appearances as a judge on a local version of So You Think You Can Dance, where she was a fan favorite for her withering takedowns and won the nick name “poisonous tongue”. Her popularity eventually led to The Jin Xing Show, a wildly successful talk show — it’s viewed by an estimated 100 million every week.

She’s probably my favorite Chinese celebrity for her unparallel courage to go through gender transformation, let alone her superb talent in dancing, hosting and language learning. That’s right, she speaks 7 languages.

【P2】人物类 话唠

Describe someone who is talkative
You should say:
Who this person is
How you knew this person
What this person likes to talk about
How you feel about this person

  1. 本题的A是someone, 描述人；B是talkative, 属性。基本思路为两类：拓展talkative属性并举例；以故事体现talkative。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 人物基本信息、外貌性格特征，引出talkative总述
    • 详述talkative的表现，或讲故事
    • 对ta的感受、评价，适当升华
  3. 时态建议：描述人物特征时用一般现在时，讲故事用过去时，最后的评价如果涉及到类别问题，可用一般现在时；述及对现在的影响可用现在完成时。
  4. 其他：talkative本身是中性词，不见得必须理解为贬义的“话唠”，也可以理解为健谈、爱说话等。描述时注意使用talkative的各种同义词，尽量避免反复。

I’m gonna describe one roommate of mine back in university, Jerry, an impressive blabbermouth长舌者, maybe the mouthiest person I’ve ever met. It’s a miracle that we remain friends, right? He is tall and lanky with huge glasses on his face, which make his face even smaller.

The story of how we met is a funny one. So, it was our orientation day, I got to see my 5 other roommates for the first time. We decided to have lunch together in the cramped campus canteen. Sitting around a table, we didn’t know how to start this conversation, cause we weren’t so familiar and things. But here comes Jerry, carrying his tray approaching. Well all I can say is that, unlike most of us, cracking a conversation with someone he doesn’t know isn’t really a task on him. He began by complaining about how hot the day was, and how tiny our dorm room was. Apparently, the girls sitting next table were the cutest he’d ever seen, and the food, tastiest he’d ever tasted. Just when you wonder where all these topics come from, he initiated a discussion about geopolitics of the Pacific. Amazing, huh?

So each meal we had together since that first day of university literally remained the same. When Jerry was not around, the deafening awkwardness kept creeping in. basically, we can’t live without him. Then one day, I know it was a bad idea, but I couldn’t help asking him about his eloquence and stuff. He told me that talking, to him, is a cakewalk, no matter with strangers or with friends & family. And for an extrovert like him, sharing everything is a must, or otherwise he would be depressed.

He’s my pal, and we’re gonna hang out no matter what, and I actually kind of like him for who he really is. As an introvert myself, he is the perfect role model I should talk to and learn from.

I’ve got the juiciest bit of gossip ever/would be boring without gossip

【P1技巧】三句话原则+公式

Make it three sentences. 这是回答P1的第一大原则。所有问题，再简单，都一定要答到三句话以上。除非只是考官随口一问的题目，比如 My I have your full name please? How are you today? 这类程序性问题。

而复杂一些的问题则需要动用四句话公式，以后会讲到。若碰到显然应该在P3出现的问题，那么就做好准备用P3的思路和方案作答，比如比较类的Please compare traveling by train and by air。这里的三句话指的不是三个句号，而是三个包含谓语动词的独立意思。如果能用连词等把三句话连成一句话，那是更好的。

三句话公式：intro + e.g.1 + e.g.2 或 intro + e.g. + outro
e.g. Are there many facilities in the place you live in?

Definitely. There are so many amenities in our community, like a swimming pool, a bank, a supermarket, a playgound and a health center (1). I feel so lucky to live here (2) and I can’t think of any other places more convenient than this one. (3)

【每日表达】习语、谚语、小幽默

be a rip-off

be a steal

这一组表达法用来表示东西太贵/太便宜非常棒，而且很日常。第一个rip off动词短语原意是指偷或骗，这里加上“-”之后变为名词，表示“简直就是在偷，或者骗”，指的是这个东西的价格太贵太离谱，就跟骗钱似的。而第二个表达法的steal原意也是偷，但这里指的是这个东西价格低得太离谱，就跟不要钱、偷来的似的。厉害吧？

What? You spent $ 400 on that damn shirt? You crazy? That’s a complete rip-off!

What? You spent $ 4 on that beautiful shirt? You crazy? That’s a steal!

bird of a different feather

咱身边总有些特立独行、思想独到的人，这样的人可以叫bird of a different feather. 羽毛不同色的鸟儿。这句表达法来自于英文谚语Birds of a feather flock together. 羽毛颜色相同的鸟儿总是成群飞舞，比喻人以群分，小团体抱团，不接受异己。

Jin Xing, a bird of a different feather, realized she was different at the age of four.
