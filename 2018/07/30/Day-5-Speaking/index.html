【P1】工具类：字典

字典作为学习工具，基本功能要清楚。科技日新月异，电子字典的新功能也要会讲。不一定要把自己塑造成一个勤查字典的好学生，但不要把自己塑造成完全没用过字典的人。

Do you prefer paper dictionaries or electronic ones?

This is a no-brainer for me. Electronic dictionaries have my vote. I mean, who would still carry those weighty tomes in your bag and go to school or work in this information age? Electronic dictionaries are convenient to use and highly portable. Looking up an unfamiliar word has never been this easy.

How will dictionaries change in the future?

I’m currently satisfied with the ones I’m using. But I do expect some fancy new features added to them. I mean we’ve already had smartphones, smart watches and the like. How about a smart dictionary with auto translation of all texts we see all day every day?

What kinds of dictionaries have you used before?

I’ve basically used all kinds of them, esp. when I’ve been preparing for this test. English-english, thesauruses, dictionaries of idioms, collocations, phrasal verbs, usages. You name it.

How often do you use your dictionary?

I use my dictionary all day every day. I’ve already been used to looking up everything I bump into in my daily life, be it unfamiliar words, expressions or idioms. I can’t live without it.

【P2】人物类：运动员

Describe a sportsperson (athlete) you admire (you know)

  • Who he/she is
  • What he/she does
  • How you knew him/her
  • What you know about his/her life story
  • Why he is famous
  • And explain how much you know about sports

  1. 本题的A是a sportsperson (athlete)，描述人；B是you admire (you know)，限制属性。

  2. 提纲建议：

    • 描述这位运动员的基本信息，外貌、性格，引出最大特征及钦佩
    • 这位运动员的人生故事，成名经历，值得敬佩的地方
    • 对ta的评价，适当升华

  3. 时态建议：描述人物特征时用一般现在时，讲人生故事用过去时，最后的评价如果涉及到类别问题，可用一般现在时；述及对现在的影响可用现在完成时。

  4. 其他：sportsperson = athlete, admire < you know, 因此说成admire比较保险。

Sports are not my thing, and I’m often met with a confused or indifferent look when I reply “I don’t watch sports” to the regular icebreaker “What’s your favorite sport to watch?”.

But I do follow some sports news especially during an Olympic season. And here’s one talented and eccentric American figure skater that I really admire in the 2018 Winter Olympics – Adam Rippon, America’s new sweetheart. He won a bronze medal as part of the figure skating team event, thus becoming the first openly gay U.S. male athlete to win a medal in a Winter Olympics.

Rippon can figure skate better than the rest of us can walk. He finished strong in his Olympics debut, scoring high points in his free skate, which helped the U.S. take home the bronze in the team event. In the final round, he did his signature move, a Triple Lutz that he executes with both arms above his head, colloquially dubbed the “Rippon Lutz”.

He’s no regular athlete, or otherwise he wouldn’t have been this illustrious. Rippon was the first openly gay athlete to nab a spot on this year’s U.S. Olympics roster — a distinction he proudly owns. Before the Olympics began, Rippon made headlines for criticizing US Vice-President Mike Pence’s homophobic views and actually refused to meet him.

In addition, Rippon’s got a wry sense of humor which can be easily seen in his epic Tweets. And in an interview, he was asked what it’s like to be a gay athlete in sports. He said that it’s exactly like being a straight athlete. Lots of hard work but usually done with better eye brows. I mean, score!

【P2】地点类：安静之地

Describe a quiet place you found

  • Where it is
  • When you found it
  • How you found it
  • How often you go there
  • What it is like
  • And explain how you felt about the place
  1. 本题的A是a place，描述地点；B是quiet, you found，限制属性。
  2. 提纲建议：
    1. 描述此地的基本信息，地理位置、样貌，引出安静的特征
    2. 详述发现经过、安静的表现
    3. 对此地的感受
  3. 时态建议：描述地点位置、样貌时用一般现在时，讲故事用过去时，最后的评价如果涉及到类别问题，可用一般现在时；述及对现在的影响可用现在完成时。
  4. 其他：此题容易跑题。这里的B是quiet，一定要重点描述quiet的场面，不能一带而过，却把重点放在此地的其他方面。

wide／cozy／simple decorated／not far away from dorm

Well, this topic reminds me of the film I recently watched about a family being forced to live in silence while hiding from monsters with ultra-sensitive超灵敏 hearing in a post-apocalyptic后世界末日 world. If you haven’t seen it, I highly recommend it. Anyway, I wanna describe a much less frightening place. Actually, a quite peaceful one – our campus library.

I still remember how astonished I was the first time I went there and found out how perfectly tranquil宁静的 the atmosphere there was. That was on the orientation day, after registering, I rushed to the library which to me was supposed to be a place of pilgrimage for university students with the help of that printed campus map. And I wasn’t disappointed. With a splendidly huge metallic dome on the top, the building is outstanding from campus architectures. Stepping inside, I was welcomed by the scent of old books and I soon surprisingly found that it was equipped with a security check machine seen in airports, several multi-media rooms, and even a book sterilizer thing, like an oven.

But the part that impressed me the most was that the reading rooms where many students chose to study. Some were reading, contemplating, writing and scratching their heads, others typing and clicking. But surprisingly, silence filled my ears. It was so quiet that you could even hear the pin drop. People searching for books were almost tiptoeing, moving swiftly from one shelf to another. It felt magical, cause outside the building, freshmen were romping around, immersed in excitement.

Undoubtedly, this library has been the place I’ve been to the most regularly, even when I don’t need or want to study. Sitting there alone, hearing the rustling of papers and smelling the intriguing奇妙的 fragrance香味 of pages with my eyes closed feels like heaven to me. Well no wonder they say Paradise will be a kind of library. absorbing konwledges

【P2】地点类：爱与恨的建筑

Describe a building you like (dislike)

  • Where it is
  • What it is used for
  • What it looks like
  • And explain why you like or dislike it

  1. 本题的A是a building，描述建筑物；B是you like (dislike)，限制情感走向。

  2. 提纲建议：

    • 描述该建筑的基本信息，位置、外观、功能，引出亮点（最大特征）
    • 详述最大特征，评价喜爱（或讨厌）
    • 评价与升华

  3. 时态建议：描述建筑特征时用一般现在时，如果要讲故事用过去时，最后的评价如果涉及到类别问题，可用一般现在时；述及对现在的影响可用现在完成时。

  4. 其他：括号为不同版本的题目，不代表可以选择喜欢或不喜欢。若抽到括号内题目，则情感走向完全相反，但中性特征仍可保留。准备时可分别储备喜欢的点和不喜欢的点，考场上视情况选取。

I’d like to describe the Beijing Olympic Observation Tower which is the second tallest tower in Beijing after the Central Radio & TV Tower, and the fourth-tallest structure in the city. It was finished in 2015, 7 years after the Olympics, and soon became a new landmark of Beijing. It’s located in the north of Beijing, being part of the Olympic Green in the Chaoyang District.

From the outside it is quite striking, a very narrow cluster of five grass-like stems or five needle mushrooms and there is nothing else around it which really makes it stand out. Its design, with four smaller circular floors at different heights and positions below a larger top floor, is meant to echo the Olympic rings.

The name says it all, and it’s used for observing the city. Since it’s near the breathtaking Olympic Forest Park, one can enjoy an incredible panorama全景 of it, the lovely lake and luxuriant vegetation, etc. Thanks to it’s height, sceneries as far as the south 4th ring road can be spotted, well, when the air’s clear.

If you ask me whether I like it or not, I’ll say I’m on the fence about this one. On one hand, it’s an amazing architecture with its magnificence and height and everything, not to mention the breathtaking view you get on the opening observation deck on top. And it’s nearly always empty with few visitors. But on the other, it takes you 138 yuan to get in, which is not at all a steal and the reason why it’s rarely visited. Besides, the facilities inside are mysteriously worn out, with a 70’s vibe.

Having said that, if you already find yourself in the Olympic Park and you have a bunch of hours to spend, then I’ll recommend it.

【每日表达】句型、习语、小幽默

A be the most B C I’ve ever D

  • 这是一个用最高级表达情感的句子。She’s the prettiest girl I’ve ever met. 简简单单一个最高级，把这里的对象夸到了极致。这种最高级也许不是真实情况，毕竟，well，你看过了许多美女，也看过了许多美景，哪有那么容易就是“最”，对吧？但是，这种最高级还是经常出现在口语表达中，借以抒发某种感情。A是对象，B是个形容词，注意用最高级，C是对象所属的类别，D则是一个动词，注意用过去分词。Be动词注意根据时态进行变换。
  • The meditation rooms in the backyard are the quietest places I’ve ever been to.
  • Jia Ling is the most hilarious comedian I’ve ever seen!

make ends meet

tighten one’s belt

pinch pennies

  • 钱从哪来？应该是很多现代人最常思考的哲学命题。在这个人类究极问题被你回答出来之前，日子好像不太好过，好一点叫收支刚好抵平，凑合能过下去，这时候叫make ends meet, 再惨一点得拮据着过，勒紧裤腰带，tighten your belt, 再惨一点，零钱都得要挤挤才出得来，叫pinch pennies。祝大家发财。
  • Living in metropolises like Shanghai is so hard. I can roughly make ends meet.
  • You’ve been doing great with your job and promotion is around the corner. But you gotta tighten your belt before that.
  • In the Great Recession, people needed to pinch pennies to survive.
  • I don’t need to make ends meet 表示我的收支很宽裕 / have a tight budget

【P2技巧】P2拆题解析法

Desribe A that B

  • 文体段+特征段+拓展段
  • Describe a popular comic actor/actress in your country.
  • Describe a quiet place you found.
  • Describe someone who’s talkative.

I’m gonna describe one roommate of mine back in university, Jerry, an impressive blabbermouth, maybe the mouthiest person I’ve ever met. It’s a miracle that we remain friends, right? He is tall and lanky with huge glasses on his face, which make his face even smaller.

The story of how we met is a funny one. So, it was our orientation day, I got to see my 5 other roommates for the first time. We decided to have lunch together in the cramped campus canteen. Sitting around a table, we didn’t know how to start this conversation, cause we weren’t so familiar and things. But here comes Jerry, carrying his tray approaching. Well all I can say is that, unlike most of us, cracking a conversation with someone he doesn’t know isn’t really a task on him. He began by complaining about how hot the day was, and how tiny our dorm room was. Apparently, the girls sitting next table were the cutest he’d ever seen, and the food, tastiest he’d ever tasted. Just when you wonder where all these topics come from, he initiated a discussion about geopolitics of the Pacific. Amazing, huh?

So each meal we had together since that first day of university literally remained the same. When Jerry was not around, the deafening awkwardness kept creeping in. basically, we can’t live without him. Then one day, I know it was a bad idea, but I couldn’t help asking him about his eloquence and stuff. He told me that talking, to him, is a cakewalk, no matter with strangers or with friends & family. And for an extrovert like him, sharing everything is a must, or otherwise he would be depressed.

He’s my pal, and we’re gonna hang out no matter what, and I actually kind of like him for who he really is. As an introvert myself, he is the perfect role model I should talk to and learn from.
