What sort of news are you most interested in?

Well, it used to be important, I mean, when newspapers remain the major, if not only,source of current affairs. But let me tell you, it isn’t any more.

There are all kinds even though I don’t read any of them. They publish most newspaperson a daily basis, but we do see some monthly ones too. Different newspapers cover different news and stories like political ones, economic ones, entertainment ones, etc.

Which do you prefer reading, magazines or newspapers?

Definitely magazines, with more vivid illustrations and in-depth reports. I mean, digital versions have never been this convenient too.

【P2】事件类：听说的轶闻

Describe an impressive/memorable story you heard from someone

  • What this story was
  • When you heard about it
  • Who told you this story
  • And explain why you were impressed/memorable by it
  1. 本题的A是story，讲故事；B是impressive/memorable, you heard from someone,限制故事特征和来源。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 做好讲故事的预备，比如从谁听说的，故事起因是什么
    • 详述故事
    • 讲述故事的有趣或令人印象深刻的点
  3. 时态建议：从别人那里听说的过程应当是过去时，而讲故事的部分则需根据故事本身的需要调整时态。
  4. 其他：这是叠加形态的事件类题目，也就是说一定会讲到两个或两个以上时间节点的事情，一定注意时态的运用。另外，不要忽视了这道题的故事特征要求，也就是impressive/memorable，那种没有情节没有转折没有高潮的故事就不要讲了。

News traveled fast and his lectures gathered crowds of thousands of Japanese looking to catch a glimpse of a Nobel laureate in person. But the messenger refused to accept a tip, in line with local practice. The first note simply stated, “a quiet and modest life brings more joy than a pursuit of success bound with constant unrest.” And the second contained a familiar aphorism: “where there’s a will, there’s a way.”

What’s most incredible about the story is that the two notes were sold for $1.8 million combined at a Jerusalem auction house last year.
