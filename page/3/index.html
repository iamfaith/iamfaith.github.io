But I read an article about a job that combines these two the other day in Financial Times. The moment I shut that magazine, I was like, how amazing life would be if I got to spend my workdays testing beds and pillows, and the overall experiences of staying in a hotel.

Basically, the bed tester is a product tester job you can literally sleep through. But there’s more to it. A bed tester needs to start with the bed and the linen, bouncing up and down and learn how to lie down to make sure the mattress has no dips, check that the edge of the bed is strong enough to sit on and that the support is evenly distributed so that couples don’t roll into each other. Then, the overall experiences should also betaken into account. From check-in service, to the color scheme of the room, sanitary**conditions of bathrooms to whether the time they spend tweaking the design of the blackout curtains is worth it. Not to mention the room service of some extravagant**hotels.

I can’t help noticing that some review sites have been recruiting bed testers. Yelp, the Internet has become a highly valuable resource for consumers looking to get a sense of a restaurant, hotel, or product they might be interested in. I myself have landed superior experiences based on those reviews and recommendations several times. Being a bed tester, I might not only get paid to sleep, but help people. How cool it would be!

Intelligence is one simple example of something that follows a normal distribution pattern: in a class, most students are of average intelligence, say iq 80, the numbers of people that are smarter or less smart than average are fairly equal, and a very small number of students are either extremely smart or extremely dumb.

It suddenly struck me that in order to be something, I needed not only toemulate my peers, but go one better.

I know it sounds cliched, but the haunting bell-shaped curve just hovers above, reminding me that I need to fight, for life is tough and every second counts.

【每日表达】句型、习语、小幽默

It takes one to know one

  • 这是个很传神的习语，讲的是只有你自己在某方面做的不好，才能察觉出别人在这方面做的也不好。俩人吵架的时候最好用了：
  • Zack: You are a two-faced bitch! 你个两面人！
  • Clay: It takes one to know one. 呵呵，彼此彼此！
  • 然后，也可以替换中间的某些词，用我们说的“偷换习语”的方式，达到幽默的效果。这个习语最重要的词是takes，比如Damien Rice的歌 It takes a lot to know a woman, 指的就是说想了解一个女人，真的需要花非常多心力（no offence!）。
  • When my sister hammered about how bad a cook I was, I was like, well, it takes one to know one.

Live and learn (you live and you learn)

  • 这个习语常被翻译为“活到老学到老”，其实不太准确。它更像“人非圣贤孰能无过”+“吸取教训”这个意思，指人犯了错或者见到了新鲜事物，感叹自己“嗯，学习了”。
  • Today I learned that Japanese dip ingredients in raw eggs. Well, you live and you learn.

【P2】物件类：丢失要物

Describe something important that you lost in the past

  • When this happened
  • Where you lost it
  • What it was
  • What you did after it was lost
  • And explain why it was important
  1. 本题的A是something，描述一个东西；B是important, that you lost in the past, 限制东西的属性和故事性。
  2. 提纲建议：
    • 简述这个东西的基本信息和重要性，讲故事的开头
    • 详述丢失的经过及后续
    • 总结经验教训，适当升华
  3. 时态建议：描述东西本身可以用现在时，讲述丢失和后续的经历用过去时。
  4. 其他：本题是物件+事件类的题目，东西本身和丢失及后续的经过缺一不可。

So I lost the diamond from my engagement ring. Compared to all we lose in an ordinary lifetime, losing a stone was not to be considered a tragedy. So, why, then, did I feel such loss?

I sulked around for days after I tried to backtrack and find it, when it didn’t appear. In retracing my steps, I recalled that I was on an unfamiliar treadmill and smacked my hand on the night that I lost it. I didn’t realize it was gone until the next morning. Then I searched under every piece of equipment at that gym I was visiting; I put up reward signs, I searched the pavement of the parking lot. This was months ago, and finally I knew it was time to replace it, and start to build new memories.

Despite my fears, my marriage seemed rock steady. I thought about not replacing it, not spending the money on something so frivolous. But I really missed my ring, missed the little stone that was in his pocket when my spouse proposed in Mauritius a lifetime ago, in another century, another millennium. I felt lucky though that I turned to him who then told me something and the lightbulb went on: The diamond is not our marriage. It has nothing to do with our marriage.

I didn’t even need to be reassured that we had this enduring bond. What I realize is that I may have lost something valuable, I did not lose something precious.

When it comes to sports, it’s always better safe than sorry, right?

not/never in a million years

Hyperbole（夸张）是英文最常见的修辞手法之一，有很多表达法都极其夸张、不符实际，但很好地表达出其中包含的实意。比如not/never in a million years，讲某个事情不可能发生，讲说它一百万年都不可能。这也是常见的表示拒绝和表示否定的表达法。这在英美人士的口语常见句型中起码能排进前50，然而咱却说得很少。学起来吧！

  • when will you consider marrying me?
  • Never in a million years.

【纠音跟读】Pitch Words

I prefer to study in the morning when most people at my age are still in their sweet dreams in the morning, right? I get to breathe in fresh and brisk air and enjoy mild temperature. besides, there are not so many distractions in the morning so that I canconcentrate harder and be more productive.

体会一下把这些词做成pitch words，用改变声调、拖长以及重读的方式，对表达有什么帮助吧。为什么要选这些词呢？选词有没有规则呢？

今日任务：

  1. 朗读以下句子，判断pitch words并使用改变声调、拖长、重读的方式体现出来。
  • The members in the society can get news easily from News Channels and thus keep themselves informed of what’s happening around.
  • Once, I attended a group discussion. Many people were not even discussing. They were just talking about NBA games. When it ended, I got nothing from it.
  • With many scientific databases, we can always find what we need by title, author or even content.
  • I enjoy biography most, because reading biography tells me a life story of a great person.
  • After understanding the things we don’t know previously, we improve.

（提示：pitch words可以为：easily, informed, group discussion, not, NBA games, nothing, always, title, author, content, biography, life story, previously, improve）

  1. 回答下列问题，注意判断出自己答案中的pitch words，并体现出来
  • Do you think people will still write letters in the future?
  • What happened on your first day of high school?
